Jung, Freud, and their split Biographical context for two influential thinkers

Carl Gustav Jung (1875-1961)

A Swiss psychiatrist who worked at the Burgholzli hospital and founded analytical psychology. His work explored complexes, psychological types, archetypes, the collective unconscious, individuation, dreams, religion, myth, and synchronicity.

Sigmund Freud (1856-1939)

An Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis. Freud emphasized unconscious conflict, repression, childhood experience, sexuality, dreams, and the clinical use of free association.

Why they separated

Jung became Freud's close collaborator after they met in 1907, but their theories and temperaments diverged. Jung resisted Freud's insistence that libido and neurosis should be understood chiefly through sexuality, broadened libido into a more general psychic energy, and gave religion, mythology, and symbolic material a larger constructive role. Jung's 1912 work Symbols of Transformation made the break unmistakable; their professional relationship ended soon afterward.
